International Efforts and Declarations

International efforts and declarations underscore the importance of forests in sustainable development. The XIV World Forestry Congress held in Durban in 2015 emphasized the need for partnerships across sectors and engagement with indigenous peoples. The Durban Declaration called for collaboration among forest, agriculture, finance, energy, and water sectors. Similarly, the UN Secretary-General's message on the International Day of Forests in 2015 reiterated the significance of investing in forests and fostering political commitment.

Focus on improving livelihoods and sustainable forest management.

Recognition of the role of the UN Forum on Forests and mainstreaming sustainable forest management into economic policies.

Agenda 21 and Combating Deforestation

Agenda 21, particularly Chapter 11, focuses on combating deforestation and promoting sustainable forest management. It identifies weaknesses in supporting forests and outlines objectives for strengthening national institutions and enhancing activities related to forest management and conservation. Moreover, it emphasizes the importance of improving human, technical, and professional skills for effective forest management and sustainable development.
