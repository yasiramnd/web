About Marine Life

The ocean covers more than 70% of our planet and is home to an incredible diversity of life forms‚Äîfrom microscopic plankton to the largest animal ever to have lived, the blue whale. Marine ecosystems provide essential services to all life on Earth, including oxygen production, climate regulation, and food security for billions of people.

  • The ocean produces over 50% of the world's oxygen and absorbs 50 times more carbon dioxide than our atmosphere
  • Over 80% of marine biodiversity remains undiscovered by science
  • Oceans provide the primary source of protein for more than 3 billion people worldwide
  • Marine fisheries employ more than 200 million people globally
  • The deep sea contains the largest habitat on Earth, yet less than 20% has been explored

Major Threats to Marine Life

Despite their vastness, our oceans face unprecedented pressures from human activities, causing rapid decline in marine biodiversity and ecosystem health. Understanding these threats is crucial for developing effective conservation strategies.
